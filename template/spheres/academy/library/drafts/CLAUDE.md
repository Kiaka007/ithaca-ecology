# sp_academy/library/drafts — Drafts

## Mode: Drafts

**Reminder:** Nothing here is final.

This is a rehearsal space for formal expression — things meant to be shared with others. The work here is finding the right way to say something, not discovering what to say (that happens in notes/ and threads/).

## Structure

- **papers/** — Research papers, articles, written arguments.
- **talks/** — Presentations, lectures, spoken forms.
- **applications/** — Grant applications, proposals, formal requests. Created via `drafta <name>`.

## Conventions

Drafts iterate. Version history matters less than the current state of thinking.

Drafts reference other spaces:
- Ideas come from `notes/` and `notes/threads/`
- Research links to `notes/research/`
- Visuals may be handed off to a design layer and return

Drafts have audiences. Know who you're writing for.

## Shell Commands

| Command | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| `drafts` | Go to drafts root |
| `drafta <name>` | Create new application draft |

## Working Here

- Rehearsal means iteration — expect multiple passes
- Clarity is the goal: what does the reader/listener need to understand?
- Drafts can be abandoned; not every rehearsal reaches performance
- When a draft is submitted or delivered, the final version may move to `library/archive/` or the relevant commission's `outputs/`
